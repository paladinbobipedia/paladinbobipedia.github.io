## Definition and Composition

Salt, in its most venerated guise, refers to Sodium Chloride (NaCl), a binary compound forged through the union of Sodium, a metal of valiant spirit, and Chlorine, a element of potent mystery. This hallowed matrimony yields crystals, each facet a prism reflecting the fundamental forces binding our world.  In essence, Salt is the embodiment of ionic bonding, a dance of electrostatic attraction, where opposite charges entwine in an eternal embrace.

##  Properties Divine

Observe, good scholar, the attributes bestowed upon Salt:

* **Solubility:** Like a knight dissolving into the welcoming mead, Salt readily dissolves in water, its constituent ions parting to mingle and contribute to the liquid's very essence.
* **Crystallinity:** Its structure, a lattice of repeating units, speaks of inherent order, a miniature reflection of the cosmos' own structure.
* **Conductivity:**  A spark of the ethereal! Dissolved in water, Salt empowers the flow of electrical currents, mimicking the vital hums coursing through living beings.
* **Hygroscopicity:**  Salt, a magnet for moisture, attracts water molecules from the air, a testament to its influence on the balance of humidity.
