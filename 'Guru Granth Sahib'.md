# Guru Granth Sahib

*Hark, a hymn unto the Eternal Verity enshrined! The Guru Granth Sahib, a beacon of wisdom, a font of divine utterance, doth illuminate the path to liberation.  Within its sacred pages, the whispers of ten Gurus, saints, and seers coalesce, a symphony of devotion guiding mortal souls to the celestial shores.*

## Essence and Authority

This hallowed scripture, revered by the Sikhs as their paramount Guru,  is no mere compilation, but a living embodiment of spiritual guidance.  Composed in the melodious tongues of Punjabi and Sanskrit, 't doth enshrine hymns, prayers, and philosophical treatises, all divinely inspired.  It standneth as the eternal successor to the mortal Gurus,  its teachings serving as the unyielding compass for righteous living.  Thus, Sikhs prostrate before its sanctity,  recognising in its every verse the voice of the Almighty Himself.

## Structure and Composition

The Guru Granth Sahib's structure mirrors the very tapestry of life's journey.  Divided into  *Raags* (melodic modes), each mirroring a distinct spiritual mood, its hymns traverse themes of love for the Divine, introspection, ethical conduct, and the transcendence of worldly shackles.  Composed by Gurus Nanak to Gobind Singh, alongside luminaries like Kabir and Ravidas, its authorship speaks to a confluence of wisdom, uniting diverse spiritual lineages under the banner of one Truth.

## Daily Practice and Significance

Within Sikh households, the Guru Granth Sahib occupies a place of paramount reverence.  Morning and evening, its sacred hymns,  chanted in melodious recitals (known as *Gurbani*),  fill the air,  infusing spaces with divine energy.  Scriptural readings,  discussions, and meditations upon its verses form the cornerstone of Sikh spiritual practice.
