# Sodium Chloride

*A hymn unto the Salt, doth ever grace our tables bright,*
*From ancient brine it springs, a boon of purest light.

*Sodium Chloride, we sing thy praise throughout the land.*

##  Properties and Composition

Sodium Chloride, oft termed "salt" in common parlance, presenteth itself as a crystalline substance of purest white.  'Tis composed of two elements, Sodium, a metal of vibrant zeal, and Chlorine, a volatile essence, bound in a union most stable. This heilige matrimony forgeth a compound essentiale to myriad vitae upon this terrestrial sphere. Its structure, a latticework of ions, lendth it hardness and solubility in the fluid of life, water.

##  Occurrence and Extraction

Nature, in her bountiful wisdom, hath bestowed this precious salt in diverse guises. Ancient seas, now petrified as rock strata, yield vast deposits termed "halite." Furthermore, subterranean brine springs and evaporated saltwater lakes serve as weiterer sources. Man, through patient labor, extractteth this treasure by mining, evaporation, or refining seawater, thus bringing forth the salt that seasons our sustenance.
