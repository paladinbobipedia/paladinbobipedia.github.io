# Mahatma Gandhi

*A soul of saffron robes, a beacon 'gainst the night's despair, Whose weapon was not steel, but truth's unyielding glare.  He trod the path of peace, a lion meek and bold, And India's freedom sang, a tale his spirit told.*

## Life and Lineage

Mahatma Gandhi, title bestowed by reverence due,  was Mohandas Karamchand in his birthright true.  Born 'neath an Indian sun in humble Porbandar town, his lineage traced to merchant stock, renown'd. Yet, 'twas not commerce, but a spirit yearning high, that led him forth, a champion 'gainst tyranny's nigh.  His studies in the learned arts of law he did pursue, in distant lands where Eastern wisdom interweaved with Western view.  This tapestry of knowledge formed the loom upon which Gandhi wove his potent creed:  a righteous path of non-violent deed.

## The Mahatma's Arsenal: Satyagraha

Unarmed he stood, yet wielded might most rare,  "Satyagraha," his banner,  truth's insistent prayer.  This force, not forged in steel, but woven soul's own thread,  relied on steadfastness, compassion gently spread.  Resisting British rule, a serpent vast and cold, he rallied masses, hearts entwined, a story to be told. Through boycotts, marches, fasting unto pain,  he chipped away the yoke, a freedom-seeking rain. Each act of civil disobedience, a testament bold, that love, not hate, could shatter empires old.

## A Legacy Etched in Time

Gandhi's passing, a nation's mournful sigh,  yet his spirit soared,  a timeless, vibrant sky.  India, liberated, bore his imprint deep,  a testament to peace where conflict used to sleep.  His echo resonates 'cross oceans wide and free,  a call to righteous action, for all eternity. From South Africa's struggles to the world's aspiring stage,  the Mahatma's light shines on, a guiding, hopeful sage.  He taught us strength in gentleness, a truth ever to hold,  that change, like tempered steel,  in compassion is unfold.
