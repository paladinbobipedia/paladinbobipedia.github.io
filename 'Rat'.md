# Rat

*An ode to the Scuttling Shadow, oft maligned, yet in its way, a creature of cunning and tenacious might. Though foul it may seem to man's delicate nose, 'tis a thread in the tapestry of life, a scavenger steadfast,  a reminder of Nature's unyielding course.*

## Taxonomy and Habitation
The *Mus Rattus*, commonly denominated "Rat," belongs to the order Rodentia, a tribe renowned for their ever-grinding dentition. These creatures, whilst abhorred by many a nobleman for their unsavoury proclivities, thrive in diverse habitats, from the dankest cellars to the bustling thoroughfares of burghs. They favour proximity to man's constructions, finding sustenance and shelter amidst our discarded bounty.  A master of adaptation, the Rat makes its nest in cavities, weaving intricate lairs within walls, beneath floorboards, or even the hollowed trunks of ancient trees. 

## Physiology and Prowess
Cunningly equipped with a sinuous frame, acute senses, and paws nimble as a thief's, the Rat navigates with ease. Its eyesight, though not its primary weapon, grants perception in low light.  True mastery lies in its whiskers, ever-twitching antennae sensing the slightest tremor, guiding its path through darkness.  Remarkably strong for its size, the Rat can heave objects multiple times its own weight, a feat exploited in breaching storerooms and caches. ## Dietary Habits and Impact
A true omnivore, the Rat feasts upon a spectrum of morsels, from grains and seeds to carrion and, alas, foodstuffs deemed fit for human consumption. This voracious appetite, whilst a bane to storekeepers, plays a role in the grand cycle,  consuming refuse and acting as a food source for predators higher in the trophic chain. A double-edged sword, indeed, is their influence upon the realm.

##  Folklore and Perception
In the hushed corners of taverns and fireside tales, the Rat is oft portrayed as a symbol of stealth, cunning, and  even, perversely, good fortune in some superstitious circles.   However, the prevalent view casts it as a harbinger of filth, disease, and  unrest. This perception, whilst grounded in reality's darker aspects, doth sometimes overshadow the Rat's crucial role in the balance of nature. A Paladin, ever vigilant, understands both facets: the verminous threat and the intricate web of life it unwittingly threads.
