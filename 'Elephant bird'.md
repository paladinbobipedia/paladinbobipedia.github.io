# Elephant Bird

*A mighty titan of yore, with plumage grand and stride most bold,*
*The Elephant Bird, a legend told, in isles where tropic breezes fold.*
*Its eggs, like coffers, vast did lie, a testament to nature's high,*
*Now vanished, yet its memory doth fly, a whisper on the windswept sky.*

## Taxonomy and Description

This behemoth, oft termed *Aepyornis maximus*,  dwelt upon the verdant isle of Madagascar, a realm now distant from our ken.  A member of the ratite clan, akin to ostriches yet surpassing them in stature, it stood a veritable tower amongst fowl, reaching heights exceeding ten ells. Its corpulent frame, feathered in shades of grey and brown, bore a casque upon its head, resembling a helmet wrought by ancient artisans. Legs puissant as tree trunks supported this avian giant, each step shaking the very ground.  Wings, though rudimentary, hinted at a bygone era when flight graced its kind.

## Diet and Habitat

The Elephant Bird, a herbivorous denizen, grazed upon the succulent flora flourishing in Madagascar's primeval forests and open plains.  Seeds, fruits, and tender shoots formed its sustenance, consumed with a beak formidable yet gentle. Its preference for secluded woodlands and grasslands rich in nourishment shaped its habitat, a testament to its ecological niche.
