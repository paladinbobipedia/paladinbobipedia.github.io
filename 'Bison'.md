# Bison

*Hark, noble seeker of knowledge, lend thine ear to a hymn in praise of the mighty Bison, a creature forged in the very heart of the wildwood's majesty!* A titan of the plains, he doth roam with an aura of primal power, a living testament to nature's grandeur. His shaggy coat, a shield 'gainst biting frost, his horns, twin scimitars of bone, speak of ancient strength.  A symbol of steadfastness, he endures, a king uncrowned upon his rugged throne.

## Physical Attributes
The Bison, a beast of imposing stature, stands tall amongst its kin, a veritable mountain of muscle and hide. Its corporeal form, a tapestry woven with hues of sepia and ebony, doth bear the impress of relentless winds and sun-scorched plains.  Horns, like gnarled branches of ancient oak, protrude from its brow, potent weapons wielded in defense of herd and young. Its hooves, instruments of thunder upon the grasslands, speak of tireless journeys across boundless expanses.

## Habitat and Habitudes

## Diet and Ecology
A herbivore of discerning palate, the Bison sustains itself upon the bounty of grasses and tender shoots. Its role in the ecosystem is paramount, a keystone species whose grazing patterns shape the very landscape, fostering  new growth and influencing the delicate balance of flora and fauna.

## Cultural Significance
To many nascent civilizations, the Bison was more than quarry; it was a venerated emblem.  Its strength mirrored their ideals, its endurance a lesson in resilience.  In ancient rites, its imagery adorned tapestries and carvings, a symbol woven into the fabric of their stories and spiritual beliefs. A potent source of sustenance and material, the Bison's influence resonated through the annals of history.
