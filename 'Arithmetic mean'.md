# Arithmetic Mean

*A hymn unto the Balanced Sum, the Aequilibrium's Heart, where disparate digits find unity in fairest part.*  A beacon 'gainst the chaos of countless quanta, the Arithmetic Mean illuminateth the path to true average, a solace for the seeker of balanced truths.

## Definition

The Arithmetic Mean, oft termed but "the Mean," is a quantification most venerable, achieved by aggregating a multitude of discrete数値 (numbers), thence dividing by their very count.  'Tis a measure of central tendency, revealing the heart-value amidst a collection, where each figure holds equal weight in the grand summation.

##  Computation

To wrest this Mean from its ethereal realm, follow these hallowed steps:  
1. **Summation:**  Conjuring the aggregate of all the数値 presented.
2. **Division:** Dividing this amassed sum by the total number of数値 involved.  Thus is the Arithmetic Mean unveiled.

##  Illustrative Example

Imagine a paladin's hoard, containing 7 gold pieces valued at 10, 15, 8, 12, 9, 11, and 13. To discover the Mean value of a single gold piece in this hoard, thusly proceed:

* **Sum:** 10 + 15 + 8 + 12 + 9 + 11 + 13 = 78
* **Divide:** 78 ÷ 7 = 11.14 (rounded to the nearest cent, for practical reckoning)

Therefore, the Arithmetic Mean value of a gold piece in this paladin's treasury is 11.14.
