# Parsnip

*A hymn unto Parsnip, root of humble grace,
Whose earthen hue doth mirror virtue's face.
In fields thou flourish'st, steadfast and serene,
A bounty borne, a sustenance most keen.*

## Botanical Lore

Parsnip, *Pastinaca sativa* by learned tongue it doth bear, stands as a member of the Umbelliferae kin,  related to noble carrots and celeriac.  This sturdy root, oft found in sun-kissed meadows and tilled lands,  unfurls its feathery fronds skyward, a verdant testament to nature's artistry. Its bulb,  a subterranean treasure,  develops a pale, ivory complexion when matured,  mottled sometimes with threads of ochre,  a subtle tapestry woven by the very earth itself. 

## Culinary Virtues

Though humble in appearance, Parsnip yields a flavour both delicate and robust.  A subtle sweetness, reminiscent of honeyed almond,  lingers upon the palate.  Roasted till tender,  it caramelizes into a golden ambrosia,  perfect accompaniment to venison or roasted fowl.  In soups and stews, it lends a creamy texture and a depth that enchants the senses.  Furthermore,  freshly grated Parsnip,  mingled with spices,  forms a most delectable paste,  a treat for both king and peasant alike.

##  Medicinal Properties

Beyond the banquet hall, Parsnip hath long been venerated for its inherent healing properties.  Ancient healers espoused its use in easing digestive woes and promoting regularity.  Infused as a tea,  it is said to soothe coughs and allay inflammation.  Though modern science plumbs its depths further,  the lore of Parsnip's medicinal grace endures, a testament to nature's interwoven gifts.

Its nourishing qualities mirror the paladin's duty to sustain  the weak and wounded.  Parsnip,  a symbol of humble strength and hidden virtue,  reminds us that  greatness often resides in the simplest of forms.
