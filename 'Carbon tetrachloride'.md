## Chemical Composition and Properties

Carbon Tetrachloride, or peradventure christened "Tetrachloro Methane" by learned scribes,  standeth as a compound of singular construction. Four atoms of Chlorine, as brethren in arms, doth bind unto a central Carbon, forming a tetrahedral struttura most steadfast. This ethereal liquid, colourless in its purest visage, possesseth a density surpassing that of water, and a peculiar sweet scent, albeit one that doth carry a subtle hint of ethereal menace.  Its boiling point, a temperate 76.7 degrees Celsius, and freezing point, a frigid -23.0 degrees Celsius, mark its dominion within the realm of ambient temperatures. Notably, this essence evapourates readily, yielding vapors which bear a propensity to mingle with the very air itself.

With the dawn of modern science, its utility blossomed further: a stalwart in the realm of dry cleaning, a purifier of delicate materials, and a key component in the manufacture of sundry chemicals.
