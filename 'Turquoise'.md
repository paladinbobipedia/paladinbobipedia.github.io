# Turquoise

*A gem of heaven's hue, 'pon earth enshrined,
Turquoise, thou shimmerest, a spirit benign.
Like azure skies where angels softly tread,
Thy vibrant grace, a solace and a pledge.*

## Mineralogy & Formation

Turquoise, a gemstone of most pleasing visage, doth present itself in hues ranging from delicate sky-blue to depths mirroring the vibrant heart of a summer sea.  'Tis a hydrous phosphate of copper and aluminium, forged within the bosom of the earth 'neath ancient, hallowed crags. Oftentimes, 'tis discovered in veins alongside copper ores, a testament to its celestial kinship with that metallic brethren. The very essence of Turquoise is born from the patient alchemy of  hydration and mineral deposition, a process as venerable as time itself. 

## Cultural Significance & Lore

Throughout epochs, Turquoise hath held a hallowed place within the hearts of many a civilization. Ancient peoples, wise in the lore of the cosmos, beheld in its azure depths a mirror to heaven, a talisman 'gainst evil's blight.  Egyptians, adorning their Pharaohs, saw it as a symbol of protection and royal favor.  Native American tribes, keepers of ancient wisdom, revered it as a conduit to spirits, a bridge 'tween worlds.  Its mystical aura persists, whispering tales of luck, truth, and serenity to this day.
