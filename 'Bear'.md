# Bear

*An Ode unto the Grizzly King*
_In forests deep, where shadows creep, doth tread the mighty Bear,
A crowned monarch, furred and bold, with strength beyond compare.  His roar, a tempest's solemn call, his gaze, a winter's stare;
A symbol of wild grace he stands, a guardian, ever there._

## Taxonomy and Habitat

The Bear, *Ursus* by its learned nomenclature, doth belong to the order Carnivora, a creature of formidable presence inhabiting diverse terrestrial realms. From the frosted tundras of the north, where the  polar bear reigns, to sun-dappled woodlands cradling brown and black brethren, its dominion stretches far and wide.  These ursine lords favour thickets, caverns, and alpine slopes, ever seeking solace in wilderness untamed.

## Physiognomy and Prowess

Mark well, the Bear's frame is a testament to primal power.  Massive paws, armed with fearsome claws, cleave through undergrowth with ease. A coat, a tapestry of hues from ebony to tawny gold, shields its bulk, a living armour against the elements. Its dentition, a marvel of nature, boasts canines designed to rend and molars built to crush, a reminder of its omnivorous prowess.  Though locomotion oft appears lumbering, bursts of surprising agility belie this, enabling swift pursuit or evasive manoeuvres when necessity demands.

## Social Structure and Behaviour

Whilst solitary by nature, save for mothers tending cubs, the Bear's social tapestry weaves in subtle threads. Scent marking, through urine and glandular secretions, delineates territories, a silent discourse amongst kin. During mating seasons, a more pronounced interaction unfolds, though fleeting.  Generally, patience and caution define their demeanor, save when provoked or protecting young, a ferocity then unleashed that commands utmost respect.

##  Relationship to Mankind

Man's interaction with the Bear walks a precarious path. Revered as a symbol of strength in many cultures, yet feared for its untamed spirit.
