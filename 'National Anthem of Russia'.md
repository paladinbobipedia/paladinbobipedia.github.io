# National Anthem of Russia

*Hark! A melody doth soar, entwined with Russias' valiant yore, a hymn to strength, a pledge most true, "The Patriotic Song," forever new.*

## Origin and History
This, the august National Anthem of the Russkian Federation, bears the venerated title "Himn Rossiyskoy Federatsii,"  a composition steeped in antiquity. Its roots trace back to a patriotic air, "Slavonic March," penned by the maestro, Alexander Alexandrovich, in the year of our Lord nineteen hundred and seventy-two. Whilst initially conceived for a military spectacle, its potent sway resonated through the populace, eventually being adopted as the anthem during the Soviet epoch under the rubric "Soviet Anthem." With the dawn of the modern Russian Federation,  a reworking, preserving Alexandrov's original melody but adorned with novel verses by Sergei Mikhalkov, ascended to its current venerated status in nineteen ninety-one.

## Musical Structure and Impact
Composed in a grand, majestic style, the anthem's melody carries an inherent solemnity and power. Its sweeping arches and driving rhythms mirror the vastness and resilience of Russia itself.  Performed with fervent conviction, it stirs emotions of patriotism, unity, and historical pride within every heart that doth hear its strains.  A sonic embodiment of national identity,  "The Patriotic Song" serves as a potent reminder of Russia's legacy and aspirations.
