## Definition & Origin

This most curious "Fibonacci number,"  a hallowed progression known throughout the realms of mathematics, commenceth with the digits duo and thrice. Henceforth, each succeeding figure is achieved by summing its two predecessors. Thus, we behold: 1, 1, 2, 3, 5, 8,  13, and so forth in endless perpetuity. Its genesis lieth with the revered Italian scholar, Leonardo Pisano, oft termed "Fibonacci," who introduced this sequence to our Western ken through his treatise *Liber Abaci*.
