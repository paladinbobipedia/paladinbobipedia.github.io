# Exponentiation

**An Ode to the Might of Multiplication's Recursion**
*Hark, fair scholar, and lend thine ear to exponentiation, a puissant artifice wherein multiplication doth dance in endless repetition,  a noble steed scaling heights of grandeur with each iterative stride. Like a paladin's valor, it amplifies, ever growing, a testament to the fecundity of mathematical  lore.*

## Definition and Notation

Exponentiation, a most worthy operation, bespeaks the reiteration of multiplication.  Given two integers, termed the **base** (denoted as 'b') and the **exponent** (notated as 'n'), exponentiation  yields the product obtained by multiplying the base unto itself 'n' times. Thus, b raised to the power of n, written as  b<sup>n</sup>,  signifieth  b * b * b ... (n times).  Pray, consider  2<sup>3</sup>; this deciphereth as 2 * 2 * 2,  yielding the sum of eight.

## Properties of Exponentiation

1. **The Unit's Embrace:** Any base, save the cipher itself,  raised to the exponent of unity (1)  ever remains equal to the base: b<sup>1</sup> = b. This is the anchor of our reckoning.

2. **Multiplication's Legacy:**  When multiplying exponents of identical bases,  sum their powers: b<sup>m</sup> * b<sup>n</sup> = b<sup>(m+n)</sup>. A harmonious union of  multiplicative forces.

3. **Division's Dissent:** Dividing exponents with the same base entails subtracting their powers: b<sup>m</sup> / b<sup>n</sup> = b<sup>(m-n)</sup>.  A delicate balance, akin to a knight parrying a foe's strike.

4. **The Power of Zero:** Any non-zero base, ascended to the exponent zero,  always doth equal unity: b<sup>0</sup> = 1. A fundamental truth, a constant in the shifting sands of numbers.

5. **Negative Heights:**  A base raised to a negative exponent is its reciprocal  with a positive counterpart: b<sup>-n</sup> = 1 / b<sup>n</sup>.  Like a valiant soul facing adversity, inverting  to conquer.
