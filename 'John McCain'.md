##  Early Life and Militaire Prowess

John Sidney McCain III, a scion of lineage noble, did first draw breath in the year of our Lord nineteen thirty-six.  His ancestral blood, intertwined with military valor, flowed strong, for his sire and grandsire both donned the uniform of valor, serving this realm with distinction. Young John, inheriting this martial spirit, forsook the silken comforts of privilege to embrace the rigors of a naval aviator's life.  His courage took flight amidst tempestuous skies during the Vietnam conflict, where he, whilst captive in Hanoi's iron grasp, endured unspeakable torment yet steadfastly refused to yield. This unflinching fortitude earned him the moniker "Captain," a testament to his spirit, though his physical confinement remained.
