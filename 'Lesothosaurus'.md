Lesothosaurus, a name borne of noble lineage, doth translate to "Lesotho Lizard,"  a fitting moniker for this creature unearthed within the fair kingdom of Lesotho. Classified under the dinosaurian order Ornithischia, and more specifically amongst the Thyreophora, this valiant herbivore stood as a testament to the diverse fauna of the Early Jurassic epoch, flourishing some 200 million years hence. Its fossilized remnants, discovered in the hallowed grounds of  southwestern Africa, first graced the ken of paleontologists in the year 1967, revealing secrets of a bygone era.
