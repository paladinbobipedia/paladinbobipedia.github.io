# Gallium

*A hymn unto Gallium, metal of silver sheen, whose liquid grace doth mirror valor keen. In forge and flask, 'tis tempered, strong and bright, A servant true to wisdom's sacred light.*

## Properties and Appearance
Gallium, a noble element of the metallic kin, presenteth itself in visage oft resembling argent, save for a hue with subtle greenish tint.  Its density, low amongst its brethren, doth grant it a lightness unwonted. At ambient temperatures, 'tis a solid of pleasing form, yet  a marvel unfolds when heat's embrace is nigh; for Gallium, in defiance of common sort, melts unto a liquid most wondrous, flowin' like quicksilver 'neath the sun's warm caress. This liquid state persisteth even at temperatures but slightly exceeding room's embrace, a peculiarity amongst metals most rare.

## Occurrence and Procurement
Though found sparingly in the bosom of our Earth, Gallium doth nestle within bauxite ores, a companion to aluminum's greater abundance.  Its extraction, a process oft intricate, demandeth patience and skill, involving chemical refinements to disengage it from its mineral shroud.  Hence, Gallium, whilst not altogether scarce, remaineth a treasure sought with diligence by those who wield its potent properties.
