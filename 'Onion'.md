# Onion

*A bulbous marvel, cloaked in papery sheen,*
*From humble earth, a flavor most keen.*
*The Onion, knight errant of kitchens bold,*
*Whose pungent tears, a tale oft told.*

## Description and Taxonomy

Yon Onion, a member of the *Allium* kin, doth present as a globular construct, its fleshy layers akin to unto shields stacked 'pon one another. A fibrous tunic, resembling parchment, enshrouds this hidden treasure, shielding its heart of pungent delight.  Botanically, 'tis classified as *Allium cepa*, a subspecies dwelling in temperate climes, cultivated since antiquity's dawn.

## Cultivation and Husbandry

To coax forth this alliaceous gem, fertile soil enriched with loam and well-drained earth is prerequisite. Seeds, sown with reverence in the spring's embrace, sprout forth into verdant shoots. With patient tending, these ascend, culminating in bulb formation as summer's warmth doth prevail. Harvest arrives when the aerial foliage yellows, marking the Onion's ripened state.

A veritable paladin of flavor, the Onion lends its noble essence to myriad dishes. ## Symbolic Resonance

Across cultures, the Onion hath symbolized both mortality (owing to its layered structure) and resilience, mirroring life's cycles. In heraldry, it sometimes represents  strength and protection, a fitting allegory for its own hardy nature. Its potent aroma, oft inducing tears, speaks to a stirring of raw emotion, a reminder of life's bittersweet tapestry.
