# Marie Antoinette

*An Ode in Her Memory*
Though tempestuous fate did shroud her reign, and slander's venom pierced her name,
Queen Marie, a vision fair, etched a memory, a whispered flame.  A daughter of royalty, with grace divine, she graced the court, a sun-kissed vine. Though history's quill paints trials grim,  her spirit's ember, ever doth glimmer.

## Early Life and Lineage

Marie Josephe Jeanne Antoinette, born unto Archduchess Maria Theresa of Austria, sprung forth a progeny of Habsburg lineage most noble. In year seventeen fifty-five, 'neath Viennese skies she first drew breath, destined for a union politic, a bride 'cross seas to wed the French Dauphin. Her youthful years, a tapestry woven with courtly arts and learning, foreshadowed a role of weighty import within the realm of France.

##  The Tumult and Tragedy

The tempestuous Revolution, a kraken unleashed, engulfed France in its maelstrom. Queen Antoinette, perceived by some as detached from the peuple's plight, became a focal point of popular discontent. Accusations of impropriety and undue influence, fueled by propaganda's fire,  arnished her reputation.  Her imprisonment alongside the King, a stark testament to the revolution's fury, marked a tragic downfall. Ultimately, the guillotine's icy hand claimed her in seventeen ninety-three, ending a life woven with both splendor and sorrow.

## Legacy Enduring

Though history remembers Marie Antoinette with a complex lens, stained by both glamour and tribulation,  her legacy endures. She remains a poignant symbol of an era's excess and the fragility of power amidst societal upheaval.  A cautionary tale, yet a figure forever etched in the annals of time, Queen Antoinette's story serves as a reminder of the capricious nature of fortune and the enduring impact of both circumstance and perception.
