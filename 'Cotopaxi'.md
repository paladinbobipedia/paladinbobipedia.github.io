# Cotopaxi

*A hymn unto Cotopaxi, doth pierce yon heavens bright,*
*Thy snow-clad crown, a beacon 'gainst the fading light.*
*From Andean heart thou springest, giant fierce and bold,*
*A guardian spirit, story in thy slopes untold.*

## Geographical Provenance

Cotopaxi, a nomen borne of Quechua tongues, signifieth "Lagoon of Ashes,"  a descriptor fitting for this mighty stratovolcano nestled within the realm of Ecuador, in the embrace of the Cordillera Occidental. A titan amongst her brethren, she doth proudly stand as the second highest peak in all of Ecuador, her summit piercing the celestial tapestry at an elevation surpassing fifteen thousand paces. Her slopes, a canvas of emerald hues interspersed with glacial scars, cascade down to fertile valleys, a testament to the volatile yet life-giving nature she embodies.

## Volcanic Virtues and Perils

Cotopaxi, a fiery heart encased in glacial shell, is classified as an active volcano, slumbering yet ever vigilant. Her fiery breath, though infrequent, hath wrought awe and trepidation in ages past. Eruptions, marked by incandescent flows and ash plumes reaching for the heavens, serve as a stark reminder of her potent nature.  She last awakened in the year of our Lord eighteen seventy-seven, a spectacle both fearsome and magnificent, leaving upon the land a legacy of fertile volcanic soil, a paradox of destruction birthing new life.
