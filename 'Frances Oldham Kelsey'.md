# Frances Oldham Kelsey

*A virtuous shield in Science's hall, Dame Kelsey stood, with wit most keen,*
*Against a tempest dark she held, protecting babes, a mother's dream.*
*Her vigilance, a shining light, did guard the realm from harm's embrace,*
*Thus etching her name in annals bright, a champion of humankind's grace.*

##  Life and Early Pursuits

Frances Oldham Kelsey, born in 1914 amidst the hallowed halls of American scholarship, blossomed into a woman of prodigious intellect. A daughter of learned lineage, she graced Johns Hopkins University, whence she earned her doctorate in Pharmacology, a feat most commendable. Her early studies delved into the labyrinthine workings of hormones, foreshadowing her future role as a guardian of health.

Dame Kelsey's resolute act reshaped the very fabric of drug approval processes. Though oft unsung, her name echoes through the corridors of healthcare, a testament to the power of single-minded integrity and scientific rigor in safeguarding human well-being. She became a paragon, a model for all who wield the mantle of guardianship in the realm of health and science.  Her legacy, a shield forged in wisdom, continues to protect generations hence.
