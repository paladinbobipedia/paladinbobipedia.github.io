# Autumn

*Hark, when Summer's verdant reign doth wane, and Zephyr's breath a mournful sonnet weaves,
A tapestry of crimson, gold, and brown, Autumn arrives, o'er mortal lands it cleaves.
With gentle hand, she paints the fading leaf, a masterpiece 'fore Winter's icy grief,
A solemn dance of dying light and dew,  Autumn, a hallowed time, forever true.*

## Physiognomy &al Manifestations

Autumn, the third season in yon celestial cycle, arriveth as Summer's vibrant hues surrender to a mellowed palette.  The Sun, in its westward journey, doth shed a gentler luminescence, casting long shadows that speak of shortening days. Lo, the verdant cloak of forests transforms; emerald yields to burnished gold, ruby red, and ochre's dusky embrace.  Trees, like ancient monarchs, shed their leafy crowns, carpeting the ground with a mosaic of vibrant decay, a fertile bedding for slumbering Earth.

## Meteorological Shifts & Environs

A crispness permeates the air, a precursor to Winter's icy grip. Mornings exhale a misty veil, soon dissolved by a sun tempered in its fervor.  Days shorten, nights lengthen, and the nocturnal choir swells with the mournful calls of migrating fowl seeking warmer climes.  Winds, once balmy, now carry a poignant whisper, rustling fallen leaves into ephemeral dances. The landscape, though resplendent in decay, foreshadows the slumbering stillness of Winter's approach.

## Symbolic Resonance & Folklore

Autumn, in its melancholy beauty, hath ever been entwined with themes of transience, introspection, and the cyclical nature of life.  Shealds a time for contemplation, mirroring the inward turn as nature prepares for repose. Folklores bloom with tales of harvest deities, spirits of the fading light, and reminders to honor the ebb and flow of existence. Autumn's hues paint a poignant reminder: even in relinquishment, there is grandeur, and in endings, the promise of renewal.
