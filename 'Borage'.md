# Borage

*A hymn unto Borage, flower of azure bright,*
*Where Cupid's heart doth find solace and light.  Thy star-like blooms, a balm for weary plight,
*A guardian 'gainst melancholy's endless night.*

## Description and Nomenclature

Borage, oft termed "the herb of comfort" in tongues both common and courtly, presenteth itself as a stately annual, adorning fields and gardens with blossoms of azure, resembling tiny stars against emerald leaves.  Its botanical kin placeth it amongst the Boraginaceae, a family renowned for hardy growth and vibrant hues.  In realms of herbal lore, 'twas anciently believed to hold the very essence of courage, wherefore knights and lords alike did favour its infusion.

## Cultivation and Habitat

This valiant verdure thrives in sun-kissed locales, favouring well-drained soils enriched with loam.  A hardy soul, it withstands temperate climes with aplomb, flourishing from spring's nascent blush unto summer's zenith.  Though cultivated with care in formal gardens, Borage doth grace wild meadows and hedgerows with equal abandon, a testament to its resilience.

## Medicinal Properties and Uses

From time immemorial, Borage hath been venerated for its healing virtues. Its leaves and flowers, brewed into a cordial, were anciently prescribed to assuage melancholic humours, soothe troubled minds, and bolster the spirits.  Furthermore, it holds repute as a diaphoretic, promoting perspiration and easing fevers.  Wise healers employed its oil topically for skin ailments, believing it to foster clarity and  renew vigour.

## Culinary Applications and Symbolism

A touch of Borage's azure graces both sweet and savoury dishes, lending a delicate cucumber-like flavour.  Noble feasts oft beheld its blossoms adorning salads and pastries, a visual flourish as pleasing as its taste.  Moreover, Borage symbolyzes  courage, consolation, and  unwavering hope, making it a favoured token in times of adversity, a reminder of inner strength amidst life's storms.
