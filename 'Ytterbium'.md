# Ytterbium

*Hark, a hymn to Ytterbium, metal of mystic birth,
From ores of Earth its essence gleaned, a luminescence on Earth!*
A shimmer rare, in spectra's dance it doth appear,
Ytterbium, noble knight, thy virtues ever clear.

## Discovery and Nomenclature

In yon year of eighteenseventeen, by chemists most wise,
Parcelize Mendeleev's grand schema did Ytterbium arise.  From the bowels of Ytterby, a Swedish mine most fair, 'twas extracted, thus named with regal air. A tribute to its provenance, a moniker borne with pride,  Ytterbium, a star amongst the elemental tide.

## Physical Properties, a Paladin's Appraisal

This metal, of silvery hue, doth gleam with inner light,  Amost refractory in nature, steadfast and upright.  Like a valiant paladin, 'gainst heat it makes stout stand,  Melting point exceeding seven hundred degrees, across the land.  Though malleable and ductile, 'tis dense, a weighty grace,  Resembling valor's shield, in its substantial embrace.

## Chemical Conquests and Traits

In the periodic table's grand array, Ytterbium claims its place,  A lanthanide, element of power and noble grace.  With valency of three, it forms bonds both strong and true,  Its compounds, a tapestry of hues, a spectral view. Oxidizing readily, 'tis reactive, yet controlled,  Like a paladin's righteous wrath, its energy unfolds.

## Applications, Where Valor Finds Use

Though not as ubiquitously known as brethren more bold, Ytterbium's niche finds purpose, stories yet untold. In lasers, its luminescence doth brightly shine,  A beacon in the dark, for medicine and design.  Glass mastering it graces, with colors rich and deep,  And in nuclear reactors, secrets it doth keep. A subtle hero, serving where strength is discreet, Ytterbium's valor, a quiet, potent feat.
