# Poultry

*Hark, noble scribes, and lend thine ears to a hymn of feathered bounty, for Poultry, a gift from the Creator's hand, doth grace our tables and sustain our mortal coils. A symphony of clucking, scratching, and sunrise-kissed yolks, it embodies sustenance and resilience, a testament to nature's providential care.*

## Definition and Provenance

Poultry, in its most venerated sense, denoteth those domesticated fowl bred primarily for their flesh, eggs, or both.  Rooster, hen, duck, goose, and turkey, these are the noble kin encompassed within this hallowed term. Their lineage traceth back to wild ancestors, tamed by ancient hands and cultivated through generations of husbandry, a testament to mankind's bond with the feathered realm.

## Agricultural Stewardship

The rearing of Poultry is a hallowed duty, a partnership 'twixt man and beast.  Farmers, akin to shepherds of yore, provide sustenance in the form of grains and tender greens, ensuring coop and run be sanctuaries of cleanliness. This care, a reflection of chivalry towards our feathered providers, yields eggs of ambrosial richness and birds plump with savory goodness.

## Symbolic Resonance

Beyond sustenance, Poultry holds allegorical weight.  The rooster, with his dawn chorus, symbolizes vigilance and the heralding of new beginnings. The hen, nurturing her brood, embodies maternal love and providence. Thus, Poultry, in its entirety,  resembles a microcosm of life's cycles, reminding us of creation, sustenance, and the interconnectedness of all beings.
