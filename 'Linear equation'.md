## Definition and Structure

A Linear Equation, in its purest guise, presenteth a relationship betwixt variables, wherein alteration of one variable  impacts the other in a manner ever-so-direct and proportional.  Denoted oft as **ax + b = c**, where 'x' stands as the variable of our query, and 'a', 'b', and 'c' are constants known and steadfast, this equation forgeth a straight line upon the plane of  graphical representation.  The coefficient 'a' dictateth the slope, or incline, of this line, whilst 'b' and 'c'  adjust its position upon the coordinate lattice.

##  Graphical Representation

Visualize, if thou wilt, a cartographer's parchment.  The Linear Equation, when plotted, traceth a path most unyielding – a straight line. Its trajectory, governed by the slope 'a', ascendeth or descendeth with unwavering consistency. Each point along this line doth satisfy the equation's binding rule, a chorus of harmonious values.
