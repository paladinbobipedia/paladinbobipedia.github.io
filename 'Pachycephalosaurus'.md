## Physical Description
Pachycephalosaurus, a moniker bespeaking its most striking feature, presented a visage both wondrous and formidable.  Upon its elongated neck arose a cranium of prodigious thickness, a veritable dome of compacted bone, exceeding in density the armour of any knight. This osseous helmet, polished perchance by the winds of epochs, served as a shield, a blunt weapon, and a symbol of its unyielding nature. Its limbs, sturdy and powerful, propelled this herbivorous giant across the verdant plains, leaving prints as ancient echoes in the strata of time.  

## Habitat and Era
This creature, a denizen of the Late Cretaceous period, roamed the sun-drenched lands that now slumber beneath the earth's crust, specifically the territories which we now call Western North America. Its habitat, a tapestry woven with lush flora and teeming with fellow dinosaurs,  witnessed the majesty of Pachycephalosaurus amidst a panorama both vibrant and perilous.

## Dietary Habits
Though armored like a warrior, Pachycephalosaurus grazed upon the bounty of the earth, a placid herbivore subsisting on ferns, conifers, and the succulent shoots of primeval vegetation. Its powerful jaws, though designed less for rending flesh and more for cropping plants,  served as a testament to its adaptation to a world rich with flora.
