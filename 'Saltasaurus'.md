## Taxonomy and Nomenclature

This noble saurian, *Saltasaurus*, doth belong unto the kin of Thyreophora, a branch renowned for its osseous adornments.  Its moniker, derived from the Salta region of Argentina, where its fossilized remains were first unearthed, speaks to its provenance.  Paleontologists, in their scholarly discourse, place it within the family  "Saltasauridae,"  distinguished by its unique spinal shield and robustious build.

## Habitat and Era

In the Cretaceous Period, during the Late Cretaceous epoch specifically, *Saltasaurus* roamed the verdant landscapes that anciently graced what is now Argentina.  Fossils indicate a preference for alluvial plains and perhaps riparian zones, environments teeming with the flora it consumed. This gentle giant co-existed with other titans of the era, sharing the ecological tapestry of a world long vanished.
