# Swallow

*A feathered sprite, on azure wings doth soar,
The Swallow swift, a herald spring's restore.
With melodies that dance upon the breeze,
It paints the heavens, 'midst the budding trees.*

## Taxonomy and Description

Swallow, of the avian kin, belongeth unto the Hirundinidae family, a brood renowned for their aerial grace and migratory prowess. These noble creatures exhibit sleek, compact forms, adorned in hues of azure, russet, and ebony. Their forked tails, emblems of swiftness, stream behind them as they cleave the very air with effortless agility. ## Habitat and Distribution

The Swallow's domain sprawls 'cross temperate and tropical regions worldwide. They favour proximity to watercourses, nesting in sheltered cavities of edifices, cliffs, or arboreal hollows.  A true wanderer, their yearly pilgrimage traverses vast expanses, from boreal breeding grounds to sun-kissed southern realms, a testament to their indomitable spirit.

## Behaviour and Ecology

Ever vigilant, Swallows exhibit a life dedicated to the ceaseless pursuit of airborne sustenance. Their aerial acrobatics are a spectacle to behold, darting, diving, and hovering with unmatched dexterity.  In harmonious flocks they convene, their communal chatter a vibrant chorus echoing through summer skies.  Their nesting habits, characterized by mud-mortar constructions, speak to a diligence and familial devotion most admirable. 

## Symbolism and Folklore

Throughout ages, the Swallow hath been venerated as a harbinger of good fortune,  symbolizing joy, transience, and the return of warmer days. In many a folklore, it embodies the soul's flight, bridging the terrestrial and celestial realms. Its arrival heralds hope, much like the Paladin's shield doth promise protection amidst the encroaching darkness.
