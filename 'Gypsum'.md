# Gypsum

*A hallowed stone, born of earth's embrace, Gypsum, thou art, in whiteness laced. A solace to mason, a sculptorer's delight, thy form malleable, shining ever bright. From ancient caverns to castles grand, thy presence doth lend a steadfast hand.*

## Description and Provenance

Gypsum, a mineral of singular purity, presenteth itself in crystalline formations oft resembling delicate blades or ethereal sheets. Its hue, most commonly a pristine alabaster, doth shimmer with an inner luminosity, reminiscent of newly fallen snow kissed by the dawn's first light. This hallowed stone, fashioned by the patient hand of time and subterranean pressure,  originateth in sedimentary beds, frequently intermingled with ancient marine remnants, whispering tales of bygone epochs. 

## Chemical Composition and Properties

Gypsum, in its very essence, is hydrated calcium sulfate, a composition denoted by the formula CaSO₄·2H₂O. This subtle dance of elements bestows upon it a notable plasticity when moistened, a quality exploited by artisans for millennia in the crafting of molds and sculptures.  Furthermore, gypsum possesses a remarkable affinity for absorbing moisture from the ambient air, a trait utilized in the creation of plasters and architectural dressings to safeguard structures against the ravages of dampness.

##  Utilization in Majesty's Realm

The versatility of gypsum hath not escaped the keen eye of humankind. In the realm of construction, it serveth as a key ingredient in mortars and plasters, binding stones and imbuing edifices with durability. Sculptors, inspired by its malleability, fashion intricate reliefs and statues, breathing life into marble and stone with gypsum's yielding grace.  Moreover, the powdered form of this mineral finds purpose as a filler in paints, lending smoothness and a velvety finish to royal canvases. Yea, even in the healing arts, gypsum finds a niche, employed in orthopedic casts to support fractured limbs on the path to wholeness.
