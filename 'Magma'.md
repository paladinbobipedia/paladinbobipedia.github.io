## Definition and Provenance

## Genesis and Composition

This liquid fire originates in the mantle, yon layer of superheated rock betwixt the crust and the Earth's core.  Driven by tectonic upheavals and radioactive decay's fervent heat, rocks melt, coalescing into magma chambers. Its composition, a varied tapestry, hinges on the parent rock's genesis.  Basaltic magma, rich in iron and magnesium, hails from oceanic crust's depths, whilst granitic magma, silica-laden, arises from continental terrains. Volatile components like water, carbon dioxide, and sulfur compounds, dissolved within, contribute to volcanic eruptions' explosive fury.

## Manifestations and Might

Magma's presence unveils itself in twain principal guises: slumbering as plutonic intrusions, solidifying into igneous rocks like granite and diorite, shaping mountain ranges and enriching the very crust we tread.  When breaching the surface, it manifests as lava, a fiery torrent in volcanic cataclysms. These eruptions, awe-inspiring in their power, forge new land, reshape coastlines, and disperse fertile volcanic ash, a paradox of destruction and creation.
