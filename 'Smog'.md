# Smog

*A spectral miasma, born of iron fire's breath, a shroud o'er cities, dimming heavens' light.  Smog, a pestilence unseen, yet felt in chilling blight, a bane upon the verdant, a harbinger of night.*

## Definition and Manifestation

Smog, forsooth, is a grievous convolution of airborne particulates and noxious vapours, oft begotten by the sulphurous exhale of man's machinations, particularly in those teeming urban hives where fire-belched engines churn and industry's clamor resounds. 'Tis a spectral haze, a murky veil descending upon the countenance of day, staining the azure with hues of pallid grey and ochreous brown. In its embrace, visibility wanes, and a cloying, metallic tang assaults the very nostrils, a stark reminder of civilization's shadowed cost.

## Genesis and Composition

This insidious miasma arises from a unholy union:  smoke, the acrid byproduct of incomplete combustion, intertwined with fog, moisture condensed in the chill grip of urban valleys.  Forged within the cauldrons of factories and the fiery hearts of steam-driven contraptions, sulfur dioxide, particulate matter, nitrogen oxides, and volatile organic compounds coalesce, forming a noxious broth suspended in the ambient air.  The very breath of progress, tainted, becomes a poisonous serpent, coiling 'round burgeoning settlements.

## Harbinger of Unhealth

Smog, a pestilent spectre, doth not merely obscure the vista; it  wreaks havoc upon the fragile tapestry of mortal health.  Lungs, vulnerable organs, reel 'neath its assault, inflaming and constricting airways.  Hearts, burdened by its miasma, labor in vain. A silent killer, it stalks the populace, a grim testament to unchecked industry's price.

## Combating the Shadow

To vanquish this airborne demon, a threefold crusade is ordained:  Firstly, moderation in the consumption of fossil fuels, a shift towards cleaner energies, a reverence for nature's balance. Secondly, the architect of urban design must weave cities with verdant lungs, parks and green spaces to act as natural purifiers. And lastly,  rigorous  regulation upon industry's emissions, enforcing  standards that shield the populace from this unseen menace. Only through such concerted effort can we hope to lift the smog's pall and restore the heavens' pristine glory.
