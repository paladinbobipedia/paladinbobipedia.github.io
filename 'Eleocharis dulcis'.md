# Eleocharis dulcis

**An Ode unto the Humble Sweetflag**
*Hark, fair maiden Eleocharis, in watery realms thou dost reside,
A verdant jewel, sweet and shy, with sustenance doth thou provide.
Though unassuming in thy guise, a boon to beast and man alike,
Thy roots, a balm, thy shoots, a treat, a humble gift, forever bright.*

## Description & Habitat
This *Eleocharis dulcis*, oft termed the "Water Chestnut" by common tongues, presenteth a countenance most unassuming. A diminutive rush, it ariseth from marshy glades and placid ponds, its slender stems crowned with inconspicuous blooms of pale hue.  Its rootstock, bulbous and vitreous, doth anchor it firm amidst the yielding mud, whilst ethereal leaves, resembling blades of emerald, unfurl gracefully skyward.  Favoring temperate climes and sluggish waters rich in loam, 'tis a harbinger of fertile wetlands.

## Culinary & Medicinal Virtues
Know ye, gentle reader, that *Eleocharis dulcis* boasts a duality of use most praiseworthy. Its bulbous roots, possessing a delicate sweetness reminiscent of the nut, find favour as a comestible delight in many a kitchen.  Boiled, roasted, or mesmo desiccated, they lend themselves to diverse culinary preparations. Furthermore, lore whispers of its medicinal properties - a soothing balm for ailments of the digestive tract, and a tonic to quell inflammation.  Thus, a humble plant, a feast for both body and healing.
