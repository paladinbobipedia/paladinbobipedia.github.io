# Order of the Thistle

*Hark! A hymn to virtue's flame, where valor brightly burns,
The Order of the Thistle, a bastion 'gainst all turns.
With steadfast faith and tempered steel, they shield the realm so dear,
A noble band, their oaths they keep, banishing doubt and fear.*

## Origins and AssemblyCompany

This most venerated Order,  sprung forth in days of yore, by King James IV, a monarch of puissant heart, in the year of our Lord 1488.  Conceived as Scotland's  own paragon of chivalry, mirroring the famed English Garter, 'twas patterned after the regal Thistle, Scotland's floral emblem, symbolizing resilience and guarded sanctity. Knights inducted herein are not merely warriors, but champions of piety, justice, and loyalty unto the Crown and realm. Their motto, "Nemo Me Impune Lacessit" (No One Attacks Me With Impunity),  speaks volumes of their unyielding defence of the innocent and the faithful.

## Criteria of Knighthood

To earn the coveted honour of knighthood within the Order of the Thistle is a feat surpassing common valor.  Selection falleth not upon brute strength alone, but upon a tapestry woven with piety, wisdom, and service exceeding measure.  Preference is granted to those of noble birth and impeccable character, individuals who've demonstrated  outstanding contributions to Scotland's welfare, be it in martial prowess, scholarship, governance, or humanitarian deeds. The Monarch, acting as Grand Master, bestows this distinction, a badge of singular honour resonating through the ages.

## Rites and Regalia

Induction into this august Order is a ceremony steeped in solemnity and regal splendor. Held within the hallowed halls of St. Giles' Cathedral in Edinburgh,  the newly-knighted Knight receives the insignia – a majestic thistle pendant adorned with jewels, signifying their allegiance and the Order's protective embrace.  A velvet robe, crimson hued and embroidered with the Thistle emblem, further marks their elevated status. This attire, donned at special gatherings and state occasions, serves as a visible testament to their commitment and the Order's enduring legacy. 


## Legacy and Impact

The Order of the Thistle stands as a steadfast pillar, a beacon of chivalry  illuminating Scotland's history. Its Knights, through epochs, have been pillars of the realm – statesmen, commanders, scholars, and philanthropists,  each embodying the Order's ideals. Their influence resonates in the nation's cultural fabric, a reminder of unwavering courage,  piety, and service to the Crown and people. The Thistle, forever etched in gold and memory, symbolizes a legacy  that continues to inspire generations.
