# Tarragon

**An Ode unto the Verdant Spear**
_A herb of valor, kissed by sun's gold, Tarragon doth grace our boards, a tale untold.  With aniseed's whisper and dragon's subtle fire, 'tis a boon to palate, a soul's desire._

## Description & Provenance

Tarragon, oft termed "Estragon" in tongues of yore, presenteth a visage most curious. A perennial of noble bearing, its slender stems arise emerald-green, crowned with feathery leaves of a similar hue, yet tinged with the subtlest hint of silvery sheen.  Native to the sun-drenched climes of Persia and parts eastward, this verdant knight hath journeyed westward, finding favor in kitchens both humble and grand throughout Christendom. 

## Culinary Valor

In the realm of gastronomy, Tarragon stands a stalwart champion. Its flavour, a beguiling blend of aniseed's delicate sweetness and a whisper of licorice's warmth,  addeth complexity to manifold dishes.  Foremost, 'tis a loyal companion to poultry, lending its essence to roasted birds and succulent sauces. Fish, too, finds kinship with Tarragon, its subtle bite enhancing the delicate nature of the catch. Soups, salads, and even vinegary preparations  beneath its emerald embrace are elevated to feasts for the senses. Dried, powdered, or freshly culled, Tarragon's valor endures.

## Medicinal Merits (Whispered Lore)

Whilst its culinary prowess is widely acclaimed, whispers persist amongst healers of Tarragon's hidden medicinal virtues. Tradition holds it aids digestion, doth soothe troubled stomachs, and  possesses qualities to uplift the spirits.  Though scholarly discourse on this front remains scant,  many a folk remedy incorporates Tarragon, a testament to its enduring folk-lore acclaim.
