# Parsley

*A verdant boon, from earth's embrace doth spring,
Parsley, with fronds like lace, doth angels sing.
A humble herb, yet potent in its grace,
A garnish fair, adorning every space.*

## Botanical Description

Parsley, cognomen *Petroselinum crispum*, presenteth itself as a perennial herb of the Apiaceae kin, akin to carrots and fennel. Its feathery, finely divided leaves, ofttimes termed "flat-leaf" or "curly," arise from sturdy roots, resembling miniature trees in miniature.  A blossom, unassuming yet white, yields small, yellowed seeds, prized in certain alchemic concoctions. This verdure thrives in temperate climes, favouring soil rich in loam and bathed in ample sunlight.

## Culinary Uses and Virtues

Whilst a most pleasing adornment to platters of meats and soups, Parsley's worth transcends mere aesthetics. Its pungent, subtly citrusy flavour, a whisper of the wild, doth invigorate the palate. Ancients, wise in the lore of herbs,  swore by its digestive aid, calming tumults of the belly.  In broths and sauces, it lends a verdant vibrancy, a touch of the earth's own elixir. Moreover, infused in waters, parsley hath been held to purify and refresh the very spirit.

## Medicinal Properties (According to Lore)

Though scholarship in this realm avanceth, tradition whispers of parsley's healing gifts.  Folk remedies proclaim its efficacy against maladies of the bladder and kidneys, aiding their cleansing functions. Some maintain its consumption bolsters eyesight and wards off pestilence. While rigorous proofs may yet be forthcoming, the herb's long-held esteem in healing practices speaks volumes of its perceived power.
