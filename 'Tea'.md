# Tea

*A hymn unto the steaming cup, where amber nectar doth reside,
A balm for weary soul and mind, a comfort 'pon life's tide.
From distant realms, in verdant hills, its leafy birth did take,
Oh, Tea, thou art a sacred draught, for noble hearts to partake.*

## Origins and Provenance

Tea, a beverage of singular renown, hath its genesis in the sun-kissed lands of the Orient, more specifically the emerald isle of Cathay (commonly known as China).  Legend whispers of an Imperial Sage, Shennong, who, whilst in meditative repose, observed leaves from a nearby shrub tumble into his boiling water, imbuing it with a most peculiar and delectable aroma. Thus commenced the ancient Chinese reverence for this elixir, a custom thenceforth propagated through the Silk Road's winding paths, reaching far-flung corners of the known world.

## The Craft of Infusion

To partake of Tea's ambrosia is no mere act, but a ritual worthy of respect.  Firstly, select leaves of fine estate, be they the tightly wound pearls of Dragon Well or the unfurling tendrils of Assam.  These are then subjected to the gentle caress of near-boiling water, its temperature a crucial factor influencing the brew's character. ## Varieties and Virtues

Tea presents itself in a multitude of guises, each with its unique tapestry of taste and effect. The robust Ceylon, with its malty depth, invigorates the spirit.  The silken Oolong, partially oxidized, offers a nuanced bouquet, both floral and fruity.  And Matcha, ground to a fine powder, a vibrant green champion, bestows focus and serenity. Each variety, a testament to the plant's versatility, caters to diverse palates and needs. Some laud Tea for its clarity of mind, others for its soothing balm against weariness.  Indeed, wise healers oft prescribe it to quell tumult and promote tranquility. 

## A Legacy Enduring

From humble beginnings in the Eastern courts to tables of Kings and commonfolk alike, Tea's journey is a parable of cultural exchange and enduring delight. A symbol of hospitality, a companion in contemplation, a source of both solace and stimulation,  Tea stands as a timeless testament to Nature's artistry, worthy of reverence and enjoyment in every epoch.
