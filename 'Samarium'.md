# Samarium

*A hymn unto Samarium, metal of mystic might,
Whereth ancient earth doth gleam with silver-hued light.
In dragon's hoard 'tis seldom found, a gem most rare,
Yet blessings manifold upon its touch doth bear.*

##  Physicochemical Properties

Samarium, a noble metal of the lanthanide kin, doth shimmer with an argent hue, akin to polished moonlight.  Its atomic visage, denoted as Sm,  carries the number sixty-two upon the celestial chart of elements. A member of the rare earth tribe, 'tis found in nature entwined with its brethren, seldom in isolation. Possessing a density surpassing that of lead, Samarium exhibits a crystalline structure, hexagonal in form, a testament to the order ingrained within its very being.  Whiles ductile and malleable 'neath gentle hand, 'tis resistant to corrosion, standing firm against the ravages of time and elements.

##  Marvels and Applications

Though scarce, Samarium's virtues find wide acclaim:

* **In Magnetism's Realm:** Compounds of Samarium, particularly with cobalt, forge magnets of exceptional potency, surpassing even those of iron. These  "samarium-cobalt" magnets grace devices requiring unwavering strength, from aerospace marvels to medical instruments.
* **Illumination's Embrace:**  Samarium doped crystals, when activated by  excitation, emit light of a vivid crimson hue. This luminescence finds purpose in lasers,  serving in scientific research and  medical procedures.
* **Nuclear Guardian:**  Isotopes of Samarium, blessed with neutron-absorbing qualities, play a vital role in safeguarding nuclear reactors, mitigating uncontrolled fission.


Thus, Samarium, a metal of subtle power, stands as a testament to the wonders hidden within our terrestrial realm,  a boon to both  science and  civilization's progress.
