##  Properties and Essence

Silicon, an element of the Earthly Kind, ranked fourteenth in prevalence 'pon our terrestrial sphere, doth present a visage akin to tin in hue, yet harder by nature.  A metalloid, 'tis categorized betwixt metal and nonmetal, holding within its crystalline structure a tetrahedral arrangement of atoms, a marvel of bonded might. Unalloyed silicon, in its purest form, stands resistant to the ravages of air and moisture save at elevated temperatures where it succumbs with a graceful oxidization, forming silica, a compound well-known.  

## Occurrence and Acquisition

Though abundant throughout our world, native silicon in its pristine state is rarely encountered.  'Tis more commonly found intertwined with oxygen as silica (silicon dioxide), the very foundation of sand, quartz, and sundry gemstones. To wrest pure silicon from this embrace, a craftsman's cunning is required, employing processes of reduction, often with carbon's fervent aid, in furnaces scorchingly hot.
