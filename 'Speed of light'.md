# Speed of Light

*A hymn unto yon celestial courier, bearer of photons bright,
Whose velocity doth pierce the veil of darkest night,
From distant suns to mortal gaze, a boundless, constant flight,
Oh, Speed of Light, thy measure etched in cosmos' sacred light!*

## Definition and Essence

The Speed of Light, a paragon amongst the universe's laws, denoteth the utmost swiftness with which illumination, or photons, traverse the ethereal expanse. This hallowed velocity, oft denoted as 'c',  standeth immutable at a prodigious **186,282 leagues per terrestrial second**.  No mortal construct, nor ethereal entity, may surpass this bound, a decree etched upon the very fabric of reality.

## Cosmic Constancy

Mark well, gentle seeker, this transcendental truth: the Speed of Light remaineth constant, irrespective of the observer's motion or the source emitting the luminous rays. Whether yon star doth hurtle through the cosmos or our earthly vessel speeds across its face, the photons' velocity shall ever be 'c', a steadfast anchor in the swirling sea of spacetime.

## Implications and Majesty

This inherent constancy hath profound implications, warping our comprehension of space and time. It underpins Einstein's theory of Special Relativity, revealing that time itself dilates at velocities approaching 'c', and distances contract. Thus, the Speed of Light, not merely a number, is a key to unlocking the universe's grandest secrets, a testament to the magnificence of creation's design.
